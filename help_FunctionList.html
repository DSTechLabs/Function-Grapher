Function Input
Functions can be any combination of math functions and operators (+, -, *, /) that operate on the variable 'x'. Spaces are ignored.

Examples:
  y = sin (x)
  y = 4*x*x - 3*x + 2
  y = cos (atan (x*x))
  y = log (sin(x) * sin(x))
  y = min (sin(x), 0.5)

Math Function Reference:

  pow (x1, x2)
  log (x)
  exp (x)
  sin (x)
  cos (x)
  tan (x)
  asin (x)

  atan (x)

  round (x)

  random (x) 		- square root of x
- natural log (base e) of x
- natural log base (e=2.7182818...) raised to the power of x
- x1 raised to the power of x2
- sine of x
- cosine of x
- tangent of x
- arcsine or inverse sine of x
- arccosine or inverse cosine of x
- arctangent or inverse tangent of x
- absolute value of x
- integer value nearest to x
- largest integer value less than x
- smallest integer value greater than x
- smallest of two values
- largest of two values
- random value between 0.0 and 1.0 given the seed value of x

All trig functions are performed with x in radians.
The word 'pi' is replaced by the constant 3.14159265359
